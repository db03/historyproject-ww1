Benzyl Bromide

First used by: the Germans

This gas was first used in March 1915 and was used to disrupt and harass enemies nearby by being an eye irritant. Without wearing a mask, a soldier wouldn't be able to continue at all. It was described once by a WW1 veteran like this: "This [...] gas made my eyes water. You couldn't stop crying - water was running from your eyes... The gas is still with me today."

Effects of Benzyl Bromide:

Severe eye irritation. Benzyl Bromide is classed as a lachrymator, which means it damages the corneas and feels much like your eyes being hit by something. In extreme cases where there is a lot of exposure to the gas, respiratory systems can be shut down (this, however, is very rare).
