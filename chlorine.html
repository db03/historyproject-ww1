Chlorine

First used by: the Germans

This gas was the first to be used that killed people when the Germans deployed it across trenches. The gas itself was a by-product of dye production from some major companies, so those companies started working with Fritz Haber (a notable German chemist) to work out how to use this gas in warfare across no man's land. The Germans had deployed 168 tons of the gas in an area north of Ypres by 22 April 1915 and it was very effective, confusing and hurting the enemy. The Germans continued to use the gas in gas cannisters throughout the war.

Effects of chlorine:

Chlorine is a powerful irritant that can cause death through asphyxiation. It irritates mostly the nose, eyes, throat and lungs.
